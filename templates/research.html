Research Behind PCOSight

PCOSight is built as an AI-powered decision support system for early PCOS risk prediction. The research integrates clinical symptoms, lifestyle indicators, hormonal markers, machine learning, deep learning, and transcriptomic insights to support explainable reproductive health screening.

Clinical Screening

Uses non-invasive features such as age, BMI, cycle regularity, acne, hair growth, weight gain, skin darkening, fast food intake, and exercise habits.

Advanced Markers

Adds biochemical and hormonal parameters including AMH, LH, FSH, LH/FSH ratio, TSH, PRL, and random blood sugar.

ML & DL Models

Logistic Regression, Random Forest, SVM, and ANN models were evaluated using accuracy and ROC-AUC.

Molecular Insight

DEG analysis, GO/KEGG enrichment, Random Forest feature selection, clustering, and PPI networks support biological interpretation.

Methodology Overview

1. Differential Gene Expression Analysis

RNA-seq datasets related to PCOS were analyzed using DESeq2. Low-count genes were filtered, count data was normalized, and significant differentially expressed genes were identified using adjusted p-value and log2 fold-change thresholds.

  • • GEO datasets used for PCOS-related transcriptomic analysis
  • • DESeq2-based normalization and differential expression testing
  • • Volcano plots, heatmaps, and quality assessment visualizations
  • • GO and KEGG enrichment for biological pathway interpretation

2. ML-Based Gene and Network Analysis

Random Forest feature selection was used to rank important DEGs. Clustering and network analysis were then applied to understand expression structure and identify biologically relevant hub genes.

  • • Random Forest-based top DEG selection
  • • Hierarchical and K-means clustering
  • • STRING-based protein-protein interaction network construction
  • • Hub gene identification using network topology
